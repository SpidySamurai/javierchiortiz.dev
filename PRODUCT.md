# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two overlapping audiences visiting this portfolio:

1. **Tech recruiters and hiring managers** — scanning quickly, need to assess seniority, stack, and personality fast. 30-second first impression matters most.
2. **Freelance clients and leads** — evaluating whether to commission work. Need to see quality, range, and enough personality to feel comfortable hiring a person, not just a skill set.

Both visit from desktop primarily. Context is professional evaluation — they're making a judgment call.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Javier, a creative full-stack developer. The site exists to establish credibility and win work — either employment or freelance. Success is: visitor understands the skill level, the personality, and the range of work, then makes contact.

The interface quality IS a portfolio piece. The way it's built and designed signals how Javier builds and designs.

## Brand Personality

Creative, expressive, bold. The work and the person come through — not a resume dump, not a SaaS template. The design makes choices and commits to them. Technical craft is visible in the details.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dev portfolio** — identical card grids, Bootstrap look, projects list with same-shaped thumbnails, nothing memorable
- **Corporate SaaS dashboard** — admin-tool energy, impersonal, could be any company's internal tool, no sense of a person behind it

## Design Principles

1. **The interface is a portfolio piece** — every interaction, spacing decision, and color choice signals craft. Sloppy details undermine the message.
2. **Bold choices, quiet execution** — expressive doesn't mean loud. Strong design decisions, executed with restraint. Commit to the aesthetic.
3. **Works at two speeds** — fast scan (recruiter, 30 sec) and slow read (client, 5 min). Hierarchy must serve both modes.
4. **The person is the product** — warmth and personality are not decoration. They're what makes someone want to reach out.
5. **Earn every pixel** — no filler sections, no decorative elements that don't carry meaning. Every component justifies its existence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target: 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Both dark and light themes must independently meet this bar.
